## Scenario: Joining a Community While Travelling

**Persona:** James — sales consultant, 43, travels to a different US city two or three times per month for client meetings. Often wishes he had a reliable way to meet colleagues or other travellers in unfamiliar cities.

**Goal:** When he arrives in a new city, know where the local community is gathering that week. Meet people at the spot, not eat alone at his hotel.

**Preconditions:**
- App installed on iPhone and signed in
- Location access set to "Always" so the backend detects location changes in the background
- Push notifications enabled
- Home city is Seattle but he travels frequently
- App is set to show spots in his current city (auto-detect based on location)

**Steps:**

1. James lands in Portland on a Tuesday evening after a long day of travel.
2. Wednesday morning he opens the app over breakfast. His location has auto-updated to Portland, and the app is showing him the **Portland community spot for the week**: The Multnomah Whiskey Library (Pearl District). It shows the address, hours, and a note: "This week's Portland gathering spot. Friday–Sunday."
3. He scrolls to the **4-week history** and sees that The Multnomah has appeared 3 out of the last 4 weeks (with different weeks showing the same venues occasionally repeating). The history also shows how many Portland app users have visited each venue: "34 users visited this week" for The Multnomah.
4. The social card shows: "Jessica (your LinkedIn connection) visited 2 weeks ago" and "8 other colleagues from Portland visited last week."
5. He clicks on Jessica's name and sees her profile note: "Always up for drinks with visiting guests. I go to the weekly spots."
6. James sends Jessica a quick DM via the app: "Hey, I'm in Portland Fri–Sun. Want to meet up at this week's spot?"
7. Jessica responds: "Perfect, I'm always there Friday. I'll bring my usual crew."
8. Friday evening James shows up at The Multnomah and immediately sees Jessica at the bar. She introduces him to 4–5 local regulars who are also app users. He spends the evening with people, not alone at his hotel.
9. Sunday he receives the weekly email showing next week's Portland spot (useful for future visits), and a note: "9 users you met this week also use the app."

**Postconditions:**
- James discovered a community gathering spot without any searching or decision-making.
- He met local professionals and regulars through the app's social features.
- He will return to Portland next month knowing exactly where to find the community.
- The venue (The Multnomah) now has predictable weekly foot traffic from app users.

**Key insight:** For frequent travellers, the app solves a different problem than for locals: it is not discovery, it is **social friction reduction**. Instead of eating alone or asking hotel staff for recommendations, James knows where the community gathers and can show up. The 4-week history builds confidence ("this venue appears repeatedly, so it's probably good") and reveals social signals ("people I know went here"). The venue benefits from predictable foot traffic: every Friday-Sunday, app users show up. Over time, savvy venue owners will realize: "If I want consistent weekend traffic, I need to stay featured in this app."
