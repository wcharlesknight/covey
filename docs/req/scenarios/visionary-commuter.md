**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Status:** Draft

## Scenario: Weekly Community Gathering

**Persona:** Maya — software engineer, 29, Seattle, mixes home-neighbourhood and office-district locations across the week. Has a core group of 8–10 friends and colleagues she regularly meets for drinks or coffee.

**Goal:** Know where her community is gathering each week, see where everyone has been over the past month, and coordinate with friends to show up at the same spot.

**Preconditions:**
- App installed on iPhone and signed in via Google account
- Location access granted
- Push notifications enabled
- Home city set to Seattle during onboarding
- At least 3 of Maya's friends also use the app

**Steps:**

1. On Thursday morning, Maya receives a push notification: "This week's Seattle spot: Espresso Vivace (Capitol Hill). Friday–Sunday."
2. She taps the notification. The app shows the selected spot for the week: address, hours, a photo, and a one-line curation note.
3. She scrolls down to see the **4-week history**: last week was Serious Pie (also Capitol Hill), two weeks ago was Café Umbria, three weeks ago was Monkey Loft. A small indicator shows "4 of your friends visited Serious Pie last week."
4. She taps "Serious Pie (last week)" in the history. A detail screen shows the venue plus a social card: "You went with Alex and Jordan. Alex posted: 'Loved the new sourdough.'"
5. Maya realizes she wants to revisit Serious Pie this week before the community moves to Espresso Vivace next Thursday. She texts Alex in the group chat: "Want to hit Serious Pie again Friday before the spot rotates?"
6. Alex replies: "Yeah! Saw Jordan's comment about the sourdough, in."
7. Friday evening, Maya, Alex, and Jordan visit Serious Pie together. While there, they see two other friends from the app also there (identified via an optional "I'm here" checkin feature).
8. On Sunday, Maya receives the weekly email recap: this week's spot (Espresso Vivace), the history of the past 4 weeks, and a note showing "7 app users visited this week's spot."

**Postconditions:**
- Maya attended a gathering with her community (not just personal discovery).
- She used the 4-week history to coordinate a revisit with friends.
- She saw social proof (other friends visited, left comments).
- She discovered which venues are becoming community favorites through repeated presence in the history.

**Key insight:** The app is not about finding new places—it is about **where the community is gathering**. The 4-week history transforms the app into a social memory: "Remember when we all went to Café Umbria? Let's go back." The weekly rotation creates predictability (everyone knows the new spot drops Thursday) and a natural gathering rhythm. For venues, the signal is clear: if you appear in the history week after week, you are becoming part of the community infrastructure.
