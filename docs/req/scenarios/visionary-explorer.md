## Scenario: Building Community in a New City

**Persona:** Priya — graduate student, 25, new to Tacoma after moving from out of state. Knows almost nobody locally, is actively building a social life on a student budget, and is eager to feel part of the city.

**Goal:** Discover where the local student and young-professional community gathers each week. Build friendships by showing up to the same spots repeatedly. Over time, become a regular.

**Preconditions:**
- App installed on iPhone and signed in via Apple account (low-friction sign-in was critical—she would have abandoned a longer flow)
- Location access granted
- Push notifications enabled
- Home city set to Tacoma during onboarding
- Several classmates and student-community friends also use the app

**Steps:**

1. Priya signs up on a Sunday evening. Onboarding takes under two minutes: Sign in with Apple, grant location access, pick Tacoma from the city dropdown, done.
2. The following Thursday she receives her first push notification: "This week's Tacoma spot: Common Cup Coffee (Stadium District). Friday–Sunday."
3. She opens the app. It shows Common Cup Coffee with an address, hours, and a photo. Below that, the **4-week history**:
   - This week: Common Cup Coffee
   - Last week: The Firehouse (Capitol Hill) — "12 users visited"
   - Two weeks ago: The Spar Restaurant (Historic District) — "9 users visited"
   - Three weeks ago: Metropolitan Grill (Downtown) — "7 users visited"
4. She notices a social card: "3 of your classmates visited Common Cup Coffee last week." One of them, Marcus, left a note: "Great open-mic setup, always a cool crowd."
5. She screenshots the Common Cup Coffee entry and posts it in her study group Discord: "The gathering spot this week! Anyone going Friday or Saturday?"
6. Three classmates reply: "Yes!" She now has a commit to go on Friday evening.
7. Friday evening at Common Cup Coffee, Priya arrives with two classmates and sees Marcus there (he's a regular). Marcus introduces them to four other regulars from the app. They stay until closing, and Priya feels like she has found her community venue.
8. The following Thursday, the app announces the new spot: The Firehouse (same as two weeks ago). Priya opens the history and sees that The Firehouse appears every 2–3 weeks, suggesting it is a popular recurring venue. She texts Marcus: "The Firehouse again next week?" He replies: "Always go back there, meet there regularly."
9. Over three months, Priya visits the same 5–6 venues repeatedly as they rotate through the history. She becomes a familiar face at each one, strikes up conversations with other regulars, and builds genuine friendships.
10. She receives weekly emails showing the history and social signal: "You've visited 12 times in 3 months. You've met 18 people through the app."

**Postconditions:**
- Priya built a social network through predictable, repeated gatherings.
- She went from knowing nobody to being a regular at multiple community venues.
- She feels part of Tacoma's young-professional and student community.
- The venues she frequents now have predictable weekly foot traffic from the app community.

**Key insight:** For newcomers, the real value is not the discovery of new places—it is the **social belonging**. The weekly spot creates predictability: "I know where to go and who will be there." The 4-week history builds emotional investment: "I've been to this place before, I'll see people I recognize." The rotating spot list ensures freshness without chaos: venues cycle back every 2–4 weeks, building loyalty without boredom. For venues, the pattern is clear: being in the rotation means steady weekend traffic, and repeat appearances in the history means loyal customers who bring their friends.

**Sign-up friction matters:** Priya abandoned two other discovery apps because they asked for too much information upfront. The Apple Sign In + city dropdown combo took 90 seconds. If the onboarding had required email verification, a profile, or manual preferences, she would have given up. For this persona, onboarding speed is the gating factor for whether the app ever delivers value.
