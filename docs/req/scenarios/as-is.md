# As-Is Scenario: Manual Place Discovery

**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Status:** Draft

---

## Scenario: Manual Discovery

**Persona:** Maya (or any of the three personas — the steps are similar for all)

**Goal:** Find somewhere new and worthwhile to go for a drink or coffee near her current location, without going somewhere she has already been.

**Current steps:**

1. Maya finishes work and decides she wants to try somewhere new within walking distance.
2. She opens Google Maps on her iPhone and types "bars near me" or "coffee near Capitol Hill".
3. She is presented with a list of 30–200 results, sorted by proximity or rating, with no way to filter for "not a chain" or "worth visiting".
4. She starts clicking individual results to read reviews. The first two she opens have 3.9 stars and mostly mediocre reviews.
5. She opens Yelp as a second source and repeats the same browsing loop, hoping a different algorithm will surface something the first did not.
6. After 15 to 20 minutes she has mentally shortlisted three places but is not confident any of them are actually good.
7. She shares the shortlist in a text thread with a friend to get a second opinion, which takes another few minutes.
8. She picks one based on gut feeling, walks there, and discovers the place she chose is too crowded, playing loud music, or closing early — details that were not in any review.
9. She ends up at a familiar fallback rather than somewhere new.

**Pain points:**

- Too many results with no meaningful signal for personal fit.
- Review quality varies and is gamed by chains; authenticity is hard to assess.
- The search process is time-consuming and feels disproportionate to the decision.
- Results are static — the same places appear week after week regardless of what she has already visited.
- No memory: the app does not know she was at Pike Place Brewing last week and should not suggest it again.
- Discovery is entirely pull-based: she has to remember to look. On busy weeks she never looks and defaults to the familiar.
- No gathering signal: Maya has no way to know "where is my community going this week?" She ends up at isolated venues or falls back to calling friends individually.

## Scenario: Traveller Discovery

**Persona:** James

**Goal:** Find a decent bar or restaurant near his hotel in Portland on a Tuesday evening after client meetings.

**Current steps:**

1. James checks into his hotel at 6pm after a full day of meetings.
2. He asks the front-desk concierge for a recommendation. The concierge suggests the hotel's own bar and two tourist-facing gastropubs.
3. He opens Yelp and searches "best bars Portland near me". The top results are the same two gastropubs plus a chain brewery.
4. He scrolls for five minutes but the descriptions all sound identical to him since he does not know Portland's neighbourhoods well enough to filter by area.
5. He opens a second app (Google Maps) and tries the same search, getting mostly the same results.
6. He asks a colleague on Slack if anyone has been to Portland recently. Two people respond but neither has been in the last year.
7. He gives up and has dinner at the hotel because making a decision feels too effortful at the end of a long day.

**Pain points:**

- No local context: generic search results are not calibrated to someone unfamiliar with the city.
- Requires active effort at exactly the moment when energy is lowest (end of a travel day).
- No way to express intent in advance — he cannot say "I will be in Portland Tuesday, give me options" ahead of time.
- The information he receives is the same as what any tourist gets; there is no personalisation to his location or preferences.
