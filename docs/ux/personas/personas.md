# User Personas

**Project:** Location Notifier
**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Status:** Draft

---

## Maya

**Role/Context:** Software engineer, 29, lives in Seattle's Capitol Hill neighbourhood. Works remotely from home four days a week and commutes to her company's Pioneer Square office on Fridays. She has lived in Seattle for three years but mostly sticks to the same handful of bars and coffee shops she found in her first few months.

**Goal with the app:** Receive a curated shortlist of bars, coffee shops, and restaurants near wherever she happens to be that week — her home neighbourhood one week, the office district the next — without having to actively search for anything.

**Pain point today:** She opens Google Maps, types "coffee near me", gets 200 results with no signal about which are worth visiting. She ends up clicking through four or five review pages before giving up and going somewhere she already knows. The discovery process feels like a second job.

**Tech comfort:** Advanced. Comfortable granting location access, managing notification settings, and connecting third-party sign-in via Google or Apple.

---

## James

**Role/Context:** Sales consultant, 43, travels to a different US city two or three times per month for client meetings — typically Seattle, Portland, San Francisco, or Chicago. He is in each city for two to four days. His evenings are free but he does not know these cities well enough to find anything on his own in a reasonable amount of time.

**Goal with the app:** When he lands in a new city, he wants one or two concrete suggestions for somewhere to grab a drink or dinner near his hotel or meeting venue — delivered to him, not searched for.

**Pain point today:** He asks the hotel concierge or searches Yelp, but both feel like they push the same tourist-facing chains. He ends up at a hotel bar by default because the alternative means spending 20 minutes on his phone after a long day of client work.

**Tech comfort:** Intermediate. Comfortable with iOS apps and sign-in flows. Gets annoyed by apps that ask for more permissions than they seem to need, so he values a clear explanation of why location access is required.

---

## Priya

**Role/Context:** Graduate student, 25, new to Tacoma after moving from out of state for her programme. She moved four months ago, knows almost nobody locally, and is actively trying to build a social life. She has a limited budget and cares a lot about the vibe of a place before committing to going.

**Goal with the app:** Discover locally-loved spots — neighbourhood bars, independent coffee shops, community hangouts — that she would not find through generic search results. She wants the app to do the scouting so she can show up having already chosen somewhere interesting.

**Pain point today:** Yelp and Google reviews are dominated by chains and tourist staples. She asks classmates for recommendations but those are infrequent and quickly exhausted. She wants a reliable drip of new options each week.

**Tech comfort:** Beginner to intermediate. She uses her phone constantly but is cautious about sign-up friction — if registration takes more than a minute she abandons an app. She responds well to push notifications when they feel personal rather than promotional.
